Question 1: Cell Phone Family Plan

A cell phone company offers a family plan where the cost per person (C) depends on the number of people (n) in the plan, given by: C(n) = 60 + 240/n, where 60 is the base cost per line and 240 is a shared data cost that gets divided equally.

What is the domain and range of this cost function in the real-world context?

A) Domain: all positive integers; Range: [60, 300]
B) Domain: all positive real numbers; Range: (60, âˆž)
C) Domain: positive integers from 1 to 10; Range: [84, 300]
D) Domain: all positive real numbers; Range: [60, 300]

Step-by-step Solution:

1. Domain Constraints:
  • Can't have negative people
  • Can't have zero people (division by zero)
  • Must be whole numbers (can't have fractions of people)
  • Family plans typically have practical limits (usually 10 people)
2. Range Analysis:
  • Maximum cost: When n = 1, C(1) = 60 + 240/1 = $300
  • Minimum cost: With 10 people, C(10) = 60 + 240/10 = $84
  • Cost approaches but never reaches $60 (asymptote)
3. Therefore:
  • Domain must be whole numbers from 1 to 10
  • Range is all values from $84 to $300

Question 2: Theme Park Pricing

A theme park charges entrance fees based on height (h) in centimeters:

  • Under 100 cm: Free
  • 100-150 cm: $30
  • Over 150 cm: $50

Which of the following best describes the continuity of the price function?

A) Continuous everywhere
B) Continuous except at h = 100 and h = 150
C) Discontinuous everywhere
D) Continuous except at h = 150

Step-by-step Solution:

1. Analysis at h = 100:
  • Just below 100 cm: Price = $0
  • At and just above 100 cm: Price = $30
  • This sudden jump creates a discontinuity
2. Analysis at h = 150:
  • Just below 150 cm: Price = $30
  • At and just above 150 cm: Price = $50
  • Another sudden jump creates a discontinuity
3. Analysis between jumps:
  • Price remains constant within each height range
  • No other discontinuities exist
