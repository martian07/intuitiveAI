Geometry

Here is the question for you guys, looking at the above bridge, what if the last cable C breaks down and you have been asked to replace that cable, so what will be the length of the cable C given that the bridge's pillar length of 70m and respective road length of 120m.

The answer to this will be, whenever you see an L shaped corner, similar to the corner of a door or corner of your brick or soap in a three-sided figure then the square of the side opposite to the corner is equal to the sum of the square of the other two sides.

In this example it will be

length of last cable2 = height2 + length2

C2 = 702 + 1202

C2 = 4900 + 14400

C2 = 19300

C = 140


So finally we got the length of the cable as 140m using the above method which is known as the Pythagorean Theorem.

To understand this one we must be familiar with Points, Lines, Angles and Triangles.

Points

Points can be anything in the universe, it could be the top of a mountain, a tip of the pen or middle of the pen, a dot on the blackboard, any tiny substance in the universe is a point.

Lines

Lines are the shortest distance between two points, in our example tip of the bridge pillar could be a one-point and the base of that pillar on the road could be another point, and the shortest distance between them is a line, so we can say that the bridge pillar is an example of a line.

Angles

Angles are formed where two planes meet or intersect and that skewed area is called angles. When we talk about planes, it could be anything with a straight surface, like a door's plane, book's plane, and when these intersect with each other or with one and another, it creates angles.

In this case, one side of the block of woods act as a plane and another is long flat wood which is intersecting each other at the top to create an Angle.


A very simple example is your laptop, the opening of the laptop creates an angle, when it is closed the angle is zero, and when it is slightly open like in the above figure then the angle is 30, and the more you open it becomes 60 and 80.


The best view you will get when you open your laptop at 90 or more, and that is why we needed to know about angles so we can deal with a situation like this.


Similarly, A javelin throw goes farthest when it is thrown with an angle of 45 to the ground.
